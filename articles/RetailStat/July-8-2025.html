Retail News & Views July 8, 2025

July 8, 2025

As reported in the Case Summary updated July 2, the Court issued an order dismissing the Chapter 11 case for Essex Technology Group, DIP (dba Bargain Hunt ), after the Debtors indicated they lacked sufficient funds to confirm a Plan of Liquidation. The Court also approved the private sale of the Company’s intellectual property to ACON Equity Partners IV, L.P. for $5 million as part of a credit bid. ACON is the prepetition lender and an indirect majority owner of the Debtor’s equity. During the proceedings, the Debtors closed all 92 stores after failing to secure a going concern buyer for the Company.

Walmart has opened its first owned-and-operated case-ready beef facility in Olathe, Kansas—a key milestone in its effort to build an end-to-end Angus beef supply chain, a strategy first announced in 2019. The 300,000 square-foot facility will process and package fresh beef into case-ready cuts that are shipped directly to Walmart distribution centers to serve stores across the Midwest. Beef will be sourced from Sustainable Beef LLC in North Platte, Nebraska, a company Walmart invested in back in 2022. The $257 million plant is expected to create over 600 local jobs.

Ollie's Bargain Outlet opened its first store in Nebraska on July 2. Ollie's acquired the Omaha location through a Big Lots bankruptcy auction. The Company will also open its first store in New Hampshire on July 17 in another formerly Big Lots store. In total, Ollie’s plans 12 store openings in July across 10 states, continuing its aggressive expansion and bringing its footprint to nearly 600 locations.

Amazon’s 96-hour Prime Day event (July 8–11), the longest in its history, is expected to generate $23.8 billion in U.S. online sales, up 28.4% from last year, according to Adobe. Clothing is projected to have the deepest discounts at 24%, with increased use of Buy Now Pay Later options. Walmart and Target are running overlapping sales to compete.

Ahold Delhaize disclosed that its 2024 cyberattack may have exposed sensitive information belonging to more than two million individuals. Potentially compromised data includes dates of birth, Social Security numbers, bank account details, health records, and workers’ compensation information. Those affected include current and former employees, as well as their dependents and beneficiaries. The company stated that customer payment and pharmacy systems were not impacted.

Kroger and Albertsons reached a tentative labor agreement with unions representing over 45,000 store workers in Southern California after more than 40 hours of bargaining. The deal includes wage increases, pension contributions, healthcare improvements, and added staffing. Final union votes are expected next week. A separate contract for 8,000 Stater Bros. workers remains under negotiation.

Publix is expanding further north with new stores in Kentucky and its first Virginia location planned for Winchester, now its northernmost store. Recent openings include Lexington, Walton, and Louisville, with additional Kentucky stores coming soon near Cincinnati. The move brings Publix closer to the D.C. metro and Southern Ohio regions.

Wegmans launched a pilot of Instacart’s Caper Carts at its Dewitt store in Syracuse, NY, marking the retailer’s first test of smart cart technology. Caper Carts are equipped with sensors, cameras, and touchscreens that let customers scan, bag, and pay for items directly at the cart, skipping the checkout line. Wegmans is evaluating whether the carts are a good fit for its assortment and customer experience, following earlier tests at three other stores using different providers.

99 Ranch Market plans to open its first location in New York City. It inked a deal with Mehran Realty Group on a lease for 44,000 square feet of space that was previously the home of a Hong Kong Supermarket (closed in 2024). 99 Ranch will use 22,000 square feet for grocery space, while the upstairs will feature the Eat Up food hall, which will have 22 food stalls and nearly 7,000 square feet of dining room space for 158 seats. The West Coast-based grocery chain, owned by Tawa Supermarket, has more than 60 locations across the country, including locations in Jersey City and Hackensack, NJ and Westbury, Long Island.

Workers at Dash’s Market , a four-store grocer in Buffalo, NY, are seeking to unionize with Workers United, aiming to improve wages, benefits, staffing, and workplace safety. Dash’s employs around 500 people.

U ber Eats added six regional grocers to its delivery platform, including Big Y, King Kullen, Vallarta Supermarkets, Superlo Foods, Lunds & Byerlys, and Foxtrot. The move expands Uber’s grocery reach across key U.S. regions and deepens its investment in local retail partners.

Nestle USA, Conagra , and J.M. Smucker announced plans to eliminate FD&C artificial dyes from their U.S. food portfolios. Nestle aims to complete the removal by mid-2026, Conagra by the end of 2025 for frozen foods and 2027 across retail, and Smucker by year-end 2027. All three plan to stop selling FD&C-colored products to K-12 schools by the 2026–2027 school year.

BellRing Brands shares fell 19% on May 6 after the Company warned of reduced retailer inventory levels impacting Q3 sales. Premier Protein was cited as the main driver of expected low-single-digit growth. Law firm Hagens Berman has launched an investigation into whether BellRing misled investors about demand and inventory trends.

Nordstrom will close two department stores by late August. One location in Santa Monica, California will close on August 26, and another in St. Louis, Missouri will close on August 24. The closures come shortly after the 124-year-old luxury retailer went private in a $6.25 billion deal. The company said affected employees will be supported through the transition and encouraged to apply for roles at nearby stores. Dick’s Sporting Goods is expected to move into the St. Louis location.

In the F21 OpCo, LLC, DIP (dba Forever 21) case, the Debtors notified the Court that the Plan of Liquidation became effective yesterday. We previously reported that the Court confirmed the Plan on June 24. The Plan provides for a liquidation following the wind-down of the Debtor’s U.S. retail operations and the conclusion of an unsuccessful goingconcern sale process. Holders of allowed general unsecured claims will receive 70% of any proceeds generated during the liquidation process, including insurance or litigation recoveries. During the proceedings the Debtors closed all 355 of their stores. For a review of the case, see our Case Summary .

Zara is closing its 28,000-square-foot store at San Francisco Centre, leaving the mall without its largest remaining tenant. The departure follows Nordstrom’s 2023 exit and adds to a growing list of retailers pulling out of the struggling downtown property. Other tenants, including Oak and Fort and AG Jeans, are also expected to leave. Zara will maintain a presence in the city with a new flagship planned for Union Square.

Lululemon is suing Costco in federal court, alleging that Kirkland Signature apparel copies its proprietary designs. The complaint references viral trends like #LululemonDupes as evidence of brand confusion and argues the copycat items dilute Lululemon’s value. The company is seeking a jury trial, injunction, and damages, claiming some shoppers may mistakenly believe the Kirkland products are made by Lululemon.

Nike reported FY25 revenue fell 10% to $46.3B, with net income down 86%. The Company flagged a $1B tariff hit and is restructuring around key sports categories while pulling back on legacy footwear like the Dunk. Price hikes and sourcing shifts are expected to offset the impact.

France fined Shein €40 million ($47.2 million) for misleading discount practices, citing violations of reference price rules. A probe found that 57% of listed deals on Shein’s French site failed to offer actual discounts. Shein accepted the fine and said it corrected the issues in early 2024.

Last week, we released a Dick’s Sporting Goods & Foot Locker Merger Analysis , full special analysis of the merger, which outlines key risks and opportunities for the merged company. The $2.4 billion deal would double Dick’s addressable market and significantly expand its global footprint, but it also brings execution challenges, regulatory scrutiny, and potential credit pressure. Our analysis breaks down the financial impact, store overlap, and investor response, including why a RetailStat credit rating downgrade is likely if the deal proceeds.

In the Party City, DIP case, the Court issued an order conditionally approving the adequacy of the Disclosure Statement. Full approval will occur if the claims of administrative and priority creditors who opt out of a settlement total less than $1 million. The settlement is necessary because the case is administratively insolvent; the estate lacks sufficient funds to pay administrative claims in full. The following recovery mechanism will apply: (i) $5 million distributed pro rata among all such claimholders; (ii) $9.8 million split 50/50 between administrative/priority claims and Second Lien Noteholders; and (iii) any remaining proceeds go exclusively to the Second Lien Noteholders. Claimants who do not opt out or object are deemed to consent to this treatment. If the claims of opt-outs exceed $1 million, the Plan cannot be confirmed. As we previously noted, the confirmation hearing was rescheduled from August 5, 2025, to August 27, 2025. See our Case Summary for full details of this case.

On July 1, AMC Entertainment entered into a Transaction Support Agreement with key creditor groups representing about 62% of its 7.5% senior secured notes due 2029 ($590 million of the $950 million outstanding) and 76% of Muvico, LLC’s 6%/8% senior secured exchangeable notes due 2030 ($337 million of the $414.4 million outstanding). The Company has the initial support of holders of 14% of its term loans outstanding under its existing credit agreement, and will need an aggregate of at least 50.1% support from these term loan holders to effect the transactions. See our Alert for more details.

On July 1, Kirkland's appointed Andrea Courtois as SVP & CFO effective July 21, 2025, succeeding Mike Madden, who has served as CFO since September 2022. Madden plans to pursue other opportunities but will remain in an advisory position until August 15, 2025 to facilitate a smooth transition. Kirkland’s stated that Courtois brings over two decades of financial expertise and is a seasoned leader in specialty retail with specifically targeted expertise in strategic financial planning and analysis and a deep understanding of asset and inventory management across prominent brands. She most recently served as VP of Financial Planning and Analysis at Francesca's, following successful tenures in financial leadership roles at La Senza, Lane Bryant, and Lands' End. See our Alert for more details.

Chuck E. Cheese launched Chuck’s Arcade, a new concept targeting adults with retro and modern games. Ten locations are now open in malls across the U.S., with more planned. The spinoff is part of the company’s $350 million modernization effort and expands its arcade offerings beyond traditional restaurant settings.

Rite Aid, DIP case, the Debtors filed motions to close an additional 237 stores, bringing the total number of closures to 1,184. The Court approved the sale of the Thrifty Ice Cream business to Hilrod Holdings L.P. for $19 million and certain pharmacy assets to KPH Healthcare Services. Giant Eagle was also selected as a successful bidder for additional pharmacy assets.

The Debtors set auction dates for 816 unexpired leases and 49 owned stores, with separate bidding underway for NexGen, the central fill facility, certain pharmacy inventory, IP, and loyalty data.

Kroger Health will remain part of Centene’s Medicare pharmacy network, ensuring continued access for millions of Centene health plan members across Medicaid, Medicare Advantage, and ACA exchange products. The agreement includes expanded pharmacist-led services such as medication optimization. The move supports Kroger’s strategy to position its pharmacies as clinical care hubs and highlights the growing role of retail pharmacies in delivering care while helping Kroger retain prescription volume from a major payer.

Jack in the Box adopted a limited-duration shareholder rights plan, or poison pill, after Biglari Capital bought additional shares of stock. The action came after Biglari informed Jack in the Box that it now holds 9.9% of Jack in the Box shares, and it intends to increase its stake. Biglari also owns Steak n Shake and Western Sizzlin, and has made attempts to take control of Cracker Barrel and El Pollo Loco in the past year.

Yum Brands is reportedly facilitating a potential merger between its Indian franchisees, Devyani International and Sapphire Foods, according to the Economic Times. The deal could involve a share swap or Devyani acquiring Sapphire’s KFC and Pizza Hut rights. Both companies stated they had no material information to disclose. The talks follow slowing same-store sales across the region.

Starbucks will award $6 million in stock grants to executive officers if key elements of its “Back to Starbucks” turnaround plan are met by 2027. Incentives are tied to performance on a new in-store service model, restaurant upgrades, food and beverage improvements, and changes to the Starbucks Rewards program.

Z'Tejas closed its last remaining restaurant on June 30, ending a run that began in 1989 in Austin, TX. The Tex-Mex chain once operated 14 locations before filing for bankruptcy in 2015 and again under new ownership in 2017. The closure was attributed to market challenges and an expiring lease.

Thoma Bravo will acquire restaurant technology firm Olo in a $2 billion all-cash deal, valuing shares at $10.25, a 65 percent premium from late April. Olo’s platform supports digital ordering, payments, and guest engagement for over 750 brands across 88,000 locations, including Denny’s, Five Guys, and P.F. Chang’s. The company will go private but retain its name and leadership.

On July 1, Alimentation Couche-Tard closed its $1.6 billion acquisition of GetGo Cafe + Markets, the convenience store division of Giant Eagle. GetGo's 270 stores are located across Pennsylvania, Ohio, West Virginia, Maryland, and Indiana. As reported in the last issue of this publication, Couche-Tard sold 35 locations to Majors Management to meet FTC approval to close the deal. Looking ahead, Couche-Tard plans to leverage GetGo's foodservice capabilities through a series of initiatives aimed at expanding offerings, to be announced in the coming months.

Buc-ee's opened a 70,000 square-foot store in Mount Crawford, VA, its first in the state. The Company is looking to add more Virginia stores in the future, and plans for its first stores in Wisconsin, Arizona, and Ohio. Buc-ee's operates 50 stores, including 35 in Texas.
